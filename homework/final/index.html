History of Chinese New Year



In folklore, during the Spring Festival, a wild animal called Nian, also called "Xi", appears in the winter in the wild. New Year's Eve appears once a year. This day is called "New Year's Eve" (the New Year's Eve), and the New Year's Eve is called "New Year's Eve" (New Year's Eve). New Year's Eve "Xi" is very fierce. In the middle of the night, he walked away from home and ate people. In order to avoid the New Year, the ancestors of Yan and Huang reunited together and sat together to resist the New Year's beast.

Due to the annual beasts appearing once a year, Yan Huang's ancestors worked out the knack of struggle in countless struggles. Although the young beasts are fierce, they are afraid of red, fire, and loud noises. Therefore, every family posted a red spring couplet at the door, lit a bonfire at the door, set off firecrackers, and Nianju saw a red couplet on the door of the house, a red fire ignited at the door, and firecrackers thundered. They would also say goodbye to the elders, and they would drive away Beast of the year.



The mainland of China is vast, and the customs of the festivals vary from place to place. Even children who are really unable to return home will report a message of safety to the family on New Year's Eve, so that the family will not worry about the Spring Festival. There are many religions in China, especially the atmosphere of folk beliefs that integrate Confucianism, Taoism, Buddhism and Buddhism. During the Spring Festival, Buddhist temples in various places hold temple fairs; there are also people who volunteer to pray to Buddhist temples, Taoist temples, temples, churches, mosques, ancestral grave tablets, and martyr monuments to pray for New Year's peace.


Various circles in China will also issue various souvenirs to commemorate the Spring Festival.
