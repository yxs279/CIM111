Mercedes-Benz AMG GT


On September 14, 2014, Mercedes-Benz officially released its new high-performance sports car AMG GT, which will be unveiled at the Paris Motor Show in October 2014 and will be launched in 2015. Its shape is short and smooth, and its movement is full of breath. The Mercedes-Benz AMG official said that the AMG GT is smaller and lighter than the SLS AMG. The length, width and height of the AMG GT are 4546/1939/1289mm and the wheelbase is 2630mm. The new car uses an aluminum chassis and more lightweight technology.


The Mercedes-Benz AMG GT is powered by a 4.0T V8 engine with both high and low power versions: the basic AMG GT has an output of 340kW (462PS) and a peak torque of 600Nm, with a mechanical rear axle differential; a high-power version. Mercedes-Benz AMG GT engine Mercedes-Benz AMG GT engine The AMG GT S has a maximum power of 375 kW (510 PS) and a peak torque of 650 Nm with an electronic rear axle differential. The new AMG GT is equipped with an improved version of the 7-speed dual clutch transmission used in the SLS. The gearbox is mounted at the rear of the car and provides both manual and automatic shifting, providing the vehicle with a 47:53 front and rear wheel weight distribution. This improved gearbox offers up to five different driving modes: Controlled Efficiency, Sport, Sport Plus, Individual and Race mode only available on the GT S. The AMG GT has a top speed of 304km/h and a 0-100km/h acceleration time of 4.0 seconds, while the AMG GT S has a top speed of 312km/h and a 0-100km/h acceleration time of only 3.8 seconds.
